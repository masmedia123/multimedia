Finding a sense of community can be found through sharing a meal. Cafeteria workers make this possible by providing the meals that students and faculty communally devour. The routine of eating lunch with friends and conversing or sharing a laugh is made possible because of the employees' hard work.

With Miami being a cultural melting pot for mainly Hispanic and Latino communities, many have immigrated to seek better lives in the United States. Thus, many jobs, such as Cafeteria Staff, have been populated by fellow immigrants, giving predominantly Hispanic students a taste of home. 

Influence of Latin American Flavors at Mas

CONVERSING INDOORS, students enjoy a socially distanced meal with their peers. Photo Credit: Michael Villa

Populated by over 1,487 Hispanic/Latino students, Miami Arts Studio 6-12 @ Zelda Glazer caters its menu to the majority. With items ranging from Asian chicken to sweet plantains, there is something on the menu for everyone's palette. Even with COVID-19 restrictions on food preparation, Cafeteria staff has found a safe way to serve hot-and-ready pre-packaged foods that fit the menu.

Mas'Hispanic Options

Maduros
FAN FAVORITE. One of the foods most frequently found in lunches at Miami Arts Studio 6-12 @ Zelda Glazer is sweet plantains, or "maduros." This sweet-and-savory side is a kitchen favorite amongst both the staff and the students. Photo Credit: Bon Appetit.
Picadillo
FOOD BREAK. More commonly known as "Picadillo," ground beef accompanies rice and vegetables in a pre-packaged lunch. With housing so many students each day, a taste of home gives them a break before coming back to class. Photo Credit: Sassy Spoon.
Arroz con Frijoles
HOME COOKED. Rice and Beans, or "Arroz con frijoles," a typical Hispanic household food, are commonly found on students' plates at Miami Arts Studio 6-12 @ Zelda Glazer. This side can be served with any main dish and continue to stand out. Photo Credit: Spoon University.

Watch How Mas Does Lunch

Mas Fridays

Pre-pandemic, Miami Arts Studio 6-12 @ Zelda Glazer is known to host MAS Friday's, a weekly lunch event. Many student performances and challenges supply everyone with a much-needed break between classes. This lunch-theatre tradition is known to be a weekly highlight, especially with many strict COVID-19 procedures in place. Not only does MAS Friday provide a delightful lunch for students and faculty, but it creates a home away from home.
